A Brief History of Bread Baking

Bread has been a staple food for thousands of years.  Ancient Egyptians
are believed to have discovered leavened bread by accident when wild
yeast spores landed on dough left out in the warm air.  The resulting
rise produced a lighter, more palatable loaf than the dense flatbreads
that had come before.

Over the centuries, bakers refined their techniques.  Sourdough starters
became prized possessions, passed down through generations.  The
invention of commercial yeast in the nineteenth century made home baking
more accessible, and the twentieth century brought sliced bread — often
called the greatest thing since, well, itself.

Today, artisan bakeries focus on long fermentation times, high-hydration
doughs, and locally milled heritage grains to produce loaves with
complex flavours and open, irregular crumb structures.  Whether you
prefer a crusty baguette, a hearty rye, or a pillowy brioche, the
craft of bread baking continues to evolve and delight.
